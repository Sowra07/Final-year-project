Machine Learning Approach to Bandgap Tuning of Perovskite Solar Cells

Perovskite solar cells are an exciting area of research in renewable energy due to their high efficiency and relatively low production costs. One of the key factors influencing the performance of these cells is the bandgap of the perovskite material. The bandgap determines the range of light wavelengths that the material can absorb and convert into electrical energy. Bandgap tuning involves adjusting the composition of the perovskite material to optimize its bandgap for maximum solar energy absorption. By carefully selecting and combining different elements, researchers can create perovskite materials with tailored bandgaps that enhance the efficiency of solar cells. This process not only improves the power conversion efficiency but also opens up possibilities for tandem solar cells, where multiple layers with different bandgaps are stacked to capture a broader spectrum of sunlight. The ongoing advancements in bandgap tuning are paving the way for more efficient and cost-effective solar energy solutions.
